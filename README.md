## Understanding Liquid Ketamine: What It Is and Its Uses

Liquid ketamine, often referred to as ketamine hydrochloride solution, is a dissociative anesthetic primarily used in medical settings. Developed in the 1960s, it has gained attention for its role in anesthesia, pain management, and emerging treatments for conditions like depression and chronic pain.

### Medical Applications of Ketamine
In clinical environments, liquid ketamine is administered intravenously or intramuscularly under strict supervision. It's valued for its rapid onset and minimal respiratory depression compared to other anesthetics. As of 2026, the FDA has approved certain ketamine-derived formulations, like esketamine (Spravato), for treatment-resistant depression, highlighting its therapeutic potential.

However, recreational or unsupervised use poses significant risks, including hallucinations, addiction, and long-term cognitive effects. The World Health Organization notes its importance in surgery but warns against non-medical abuse.
